IIDA DIGESTIVE HEALTH CLINIC

Nexus (Transparency & Closeness)

Transparency and closeness through design and movement shape this unique medical clinic by enveloping the patients in feelings of security and reassurance. The design approaches the concrete spatial elements and abstract user experience. The design approach in patients involvement contain visual openness, interaction with staff, closeness to children and to other patients. These reassurances decrease patient anxiety allowing for focused healing.

Home away from Home

The infusion suite brings with it the responsibility providing the patients a home environment experience of the space in which treatment occurs. The curved partitions made of walnut wood, embrace the infusion room to create a spacious, yet intimate atmosphere avoiding the cold character of many clinical environments. Facing windows further contribute to the open impression of the main treatment room by providing calming view and admitting natural light.

The Journey

Visitors entering NDC clinic instantly know something is going to be different. The space is designed with a centralized public area which progresses to the left of the elevators to a semi-private area for the reception space constructed with overlapping wooden panels that allows interaction between staff, doctors and patients. To the right of the elevators it increases privacy at the infusion suite which provides individual treatment spaces.

To Make an Entrance

Keeping with the vision of spa, our reception will set the scene with overlapping wooden panels. Each and everyone of our patients and their family members arrive to a welcoming “living room” with a variety of comfortable seating options. Our reception clearly establishes the sanctuary-like nature of the health clinic.

Disconnect

The staff lounge design intends to offer a relaxing environment within the clinic. The space offers a variety of comfortable seating options that function for several activities such as napping, meeting and eating. The translucent glass with overlapping bamboo sticks gives a sense of privacy, while maintaining an openness to the infusion suite area, which provides a sense that the doctors and nurses still have a connection with the patients.
